Introduction to BTC

BTC is a mix of Physical and Digital classrooms.

It takes online learning and combines it with classroom experience.​

Why to Re-Imagining Education Post Pandemic

" Online education is the future but ‘Lacks The Physical Touch "​

  • Pandemic has upended the education system and hence, classrooms have shifted to computer screens.
  • 1 to 1 connect with peers and teachers have completely vanished from a student’s journey.
  • Teachers could hardly tell how well the students comprehend and understand the concepts.
Benefits of Physical Learning
  • Forms Diverse Perspective
  • Establishes 1 to 1 Connect
  • Provides Real Time Doubt Solving
  • Inculcates the Habit of Brainstorming
  • Develops Logic & Reasoning Skills
  • Students Become Better Team Players

How did we do it?

Planning and Documentation
Planning and Documentation

Sprint Plan

Sprint planning sessions were done once in 2 weeks on Wednesdays. Later, the sprints were aligned to monthly development sprints.

Design Files

Each new sprint was taken up in a new Figma file, so that the development team can follow only the changes required. In some cases, if advised by the Product Team, the iterations were done on the same file.

Timesheets

Timesheets were recorded by each designer. Regular updates were shared via email to the concerned stakeholders to ensure efficient utilization of resources.
Team allocation
Initially the engagement started with 2 designers. But as the scope increased to accommodate requirements of multiple product teams (pods), dedicated resources were assigned to each pod. If work load on a particular pod was high, team members from other pods would be brought in to handle the priority after consultation with the product teams.

Methodology & Design Process

Requirement Gathering

  • Multiple rounds of discussions with Project Manager to understand the requirement brief and feedback from the students/parents having problems with the existing system.
  • Studying the existing set of design systems to understand the UI expectations and identifying the loopholes.
  • Identifying possible solutions based on day - day client interactions as well as our analysis.

Research/Benchmarking

  • The research process involves defining the problem, collecting relevant data, analyzing the data, and drawing conclusions to inform decision-making.

Ideate

  • Brainstorming and experimenting with various creative ideas to develop innovative solutions that could effectively address the challenges faced by Students.
  • In this phase we collaborated with the stakeholders to ensure that the final product aligned with the goals of the business and the needs of its users.

Wireframing

  • Choosing task flows based on priority & deadlines, breaking them into small chunks of user stories and Content
  • Blocking & Drawing high fidelity wireframes.Having multiple sessions of feedback followed with day-day iterations to arrive at a conclusion.
  • Finalising the basic structure.

Visual Design

  • Putting out first set of explorations in UI designs.
  • Getting them reviewed by the product manager.

Feedback & Iterations

  • Reiterating as per feedback from all the stakeholders on a daily basis.
  • Implementing additional requirements within the module.
