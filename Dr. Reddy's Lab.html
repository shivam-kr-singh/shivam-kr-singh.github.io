Migraine and Epilepsy Tracker

Dashboard Design for Migraine & Epilepsy Patient and Doctor's Dashboard

Project Overview
The UX case study for Dr. Reddy Lab extends beyond the existing medical diagnostic application to design separate app dashboards for migraine and epilepsy patients, as well as a web dashboard for doctors. The project aims to create user-friendly and personalized interfaces for patients to manage their conditions effectively and provide doctors with comprehensive tools to monitor and treat their patients.
The Product
    Migraine Patient Dashboard
    The dashboard for migraine patients allows them to track their migraine episodes, record symptoms, and access personalized treatment plans. It provides insights into triggers and trends to manage migraines proactively.
    Epilepsy Patient Dashboard
    The epilepsy patient dashboard empowers patients to record seizure occurrences, medication intake, and overall well-being. It offers seizure forecasts and medication reminders to improve seizure management.
    Doctor Dashboard
    The web dashboard for doctors provides a comprehensive view of their patients' migraine and epilepsy data. It facilitates personalized treatment planning, enables remote patient monitoring, and allows efficient communication with patients.
The Problem
Both migraine and epilepsy patients face challenges in effectively managing their conditions. The current applications lack personalized features, making it difficult for patients to track symptoms and access relevant information. Additionally, doctors often struggle to monitor patients remotely and tailor treatments based on individual patient needs.
The Goal
The goal of this case study is to design intuitive app dashboards for migraine and epilepsy patients and a web dashboard for doctors. The aim is to enhance patient engagement, improve condition management, and enable doctors to provide personalized and efficient care.
My Role and Responsibilities
As a UX and Data Designer, I am responsible to design all three dashboards by collaborating with stakeholders to ensure user-centric solutions and seamless integration with the existing Dr. Reddy Lab application. My primary responsibility is to conduct thorough user research, create user personas specific to each condition, design user flows, wireframes, and interactive prototypes.

Understanding the User

User Personas and Their User Journey Map
Pain Points from the User Journey Map

Migraine Patient

  • Difficulty in managing her migraine episodes amidst busy work and family responsibilities.
  • Limited time to regularly record and track her migraine episodes and symptoms.
  • Lack of personalized insights and treatment recommendations for her specific migraine condition.

Epilepsy Patient

  • Difficulty in remembering medication timings consistently.
  • Lack of insights into seizure patterns, leading to challenges in anticipating and managing seizures.
  • Limited access to relevant information and support resources for epilepsy management.

Doctor

  • Challenges in efficiently monitoring and managing a large number of patients remotely.
  • Time-consuming treatment planning, especially for patients with complex conditions.
  • Limited access to actionable patient data for making informed decisions.

Impact and Learning

  • The app dashboards for migraine and epilepsy patients, along with the web dashboard for doctors, significantly improved patient engagement and condition management.
  • Patients found it easier to track their symptoms and adhere to treatment plans, leading to better health outcomes.
  • Doctors benefited from streamlined patient monitoring and efficient treatment planning, resulting in enhanced patient care.
